# The Bible, Book by Book
### A Summary and Outline of All 66 Books (ESV Structure, Genesis–Revelation)

Chapter references follow the English Standard Version. Summaries are original; outlines group chapters into the book's natural sections.

---

# THE OLD TESTAMENT

## The Pentateuch (Books of Moses)

---

### 1. Genesis (50 chapters)
**Summary:** The book of beginnings — creation, the fall of humanity, the flood, and God's covenant promises to Abraham, Isaac, Jacob, and Joseph. God chooses one family through whom He will bless all nations.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–2** — Creation of the world and humanity; the garden of Eden
- **Ch. 3–5** — The fall; Cain and Abel; genealogy from Adam to Noah
- **Ch. 6–9** — The flood; God's covenant with Noah (rainbow sign)
- **Ch. 10–11** — Table of nations; the tower of Babel
- **Ch. 12–25** — Abraham: call and covenant (12, 15, 17), Sodom and Gomorrah (18–19), birth of Isaac (21), the near-sacrifice of Isaac (22)
- **Ch. 25–28** — Isaac and his sons; Jacob steals Esau's blessing (27)
- **Ch. 28–36** — Jacob: ladder vision (28), Laban and marriages (29–31), wrestling with God, renamed Israel (32)
- **Ch. 37–50** — Joseph: sold into slavery (37), rise in Egypt (39–41), reconciliation with brothers (42–45), Israel settles in Egypt (46–50)

---

### 2. Exodus (40 chapters)
**Summary:** God redeems Israel from slavery in Egypt through Moses, makes a covenant with them at Sinai, gives the Law, and comes to dwell among them in the tabernacle.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–2** — Israel enslaved; birth and early life of Moses
- **Ch. 3–4** — The burning bush; Moses' call
- **Ch. 5–11** — Confrontation with Pharaoh; the ten plagues
- **Ch. 12–13** — The Passover and the exodus from Egypt
- **Ch. 14–15** — Crossing the Red Sea; the song of Moses
- **Ch. 16–18** — Wilderness provision: manna, water, Jethro's counsel
- **Ch. 19–24** — Sinai covenant; the Ten Commandments (20); the Book of the Covenant
- **Ch. 25–31** — Instructions for the tabernacle and priesthood
- **Ch. 32–34** — The golden calf; covenant renewal; God's self-revelation (34:6–7)
- **Ch. 35–40** — Tabernacle built; God's glory fills it

---

### 3. Leviticus (27 chapters)
**Summary:** A holy God teaches His people how to live in His presence — through sacrifices, a priesthood, purity laws, and festivals. Theme: "Be holy, for I am holy."

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–7** — The five offerings: burnt, grain, peace, sin, guilt
- **Ch. 8–10** — Ordination of Aaron's priesthood; Nadab and Abihu's judgment
- **Ch. 11–15** — Clean and unclean: food, childbirth, skin disease, discharges
- **Ch. 16** — The Day of Atonement (Yom Kippur)
- **Ch. 17–22** — The Holiness Code: blood, sexual ethics, love of neighbor (19:18), priestly conduct
- **Ch. 23–25** — Feasts of the LORD; Sabbath year and Jubilee
- **Ch. 26–27** — Covenant blessings and curses; vows

---

### 4. Numbers (36 chapters)
**Summary:** Israel journeys from Sinai toward Canaan but rebels in unbelief; a whole generation dies in the wilderness over 40 years, yet God faithfully preserves the nation and brings the next generation to the edge of the promised land.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–10** — Census and organization at Sinai; Nazirite vow (6); departure
- **Ch. 11–12** — Complaints; Miriam and Aaron oppose Moses
- **Ch. 13–14** — The twelve spies; Israel refuses to enter the land; 40-year judgment
- **Ch. 15–19** — Korah's rebellion (16); Aaron's staff buds; priestly duties
- **Ch. 20–21** — Moses strikes the rock; the bronze serpent (21)
- **Ch. 22–24** — Balaam and his oracles of blessing
- **Ch. 25** — Sin at Peor
- **Ch. 26–30** — Second census (new generation); daughters of Zelophehad; vows
- **Ch. 31–36** — War with Midian; Transjordan tribes; boundaries and cities of refuge

---

### 5. Deuteronomy (34 chapters)
**Summary:** Moses' farewell sermons on the plains of Moab: he retells Israel's story, restates the Law for the new generation, calls for wholehearted love and obedience, and dies within sight of the land.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–4** — First sermon: review of the wilderness journey
- **Ch. 5–11** — Second sermon begins: Ten Commandments restated (5); the Shema — "Love the LORD your God" (6:4–5); warnings and remembrance
- **Ch. 12–26** — Covenant stipulations: worship, festivals, kings (17), prophets (18), justice, social ethics
- **Ch. 27–28** — Blessings and curses at Ebal and Gerizim
- **Ch. 29–30** — Covenant renewal; the choice of life or death (30:19)
- **Ch. 31–34** — Joshua commissioned; the song of Moses (32); Moses blesses the tribes and dies (34)

---

## The Historical Books

---

### 6. Joshua (24 chapters)
**Summary:** Under Joshua, Israel crosses the Jordan, conquers Canaan, and divides the land among the tribes — God keeping every promise He made.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–5** — Commissioning of Joshua; Rahab and the spies (2); crossing the Jordan (3–4)
- **Ch. 6–8** — Fall of Jericho (6); Achan's sin and Ai (7–8)
- **Ch. 9–12** — Gibeonite treaty; southern and northern campaigns; list of defeated kings
- **Ch. 13–21** — Division of the land among the tribes; cities of refuge
- **Ch. 22–24** — Altar dispute; Joshua's farewell — "Choose this day whom you will serve" (24:15)

---

### 7. Judges (21 chapters)
**Summary:** After Joshua, Israel spirals through repeated cycles of sin, oppression, crying out, and deliverance through judges. The refrain: "Everyone did what was right in his own eyes."

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–3:6** — Incomplete conquest; the cycle introduced
- **Ch. 3:7–16** — The judges: Othniel, Ehud, Deborah and Barak (4–5), Gideon (6–8), Abimelech (9), Jephthah (10–12), Samson (13–16)
- **Ch. 17–21** — Israel's moral collapse: Micah's idols, the Danite migration, the outrage at Gibeah and civil war

---

### 8. Ruth (4 chapters)
**Summary:** During the time of the judges, a Moabite widow's loyalty to her Israelite mother-in-law leads to redemption through Boaz — and to the family line of King David.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1** — Famine, loss, and Ruth's pledge to Naomi
- **Ch. 2** — Ruth gleans in Boaz's field
- **Ch. 3** — Ruth's appeal at the threshing floor
- **Ch. 4** — Boaz redeems; Ruth marries; genealogy to David

---

### 9. 1 Samuel (31 chapters)
**Summary:** Israel transitions from judges to monarchy: Samuel the prophet anoints Saul, Saul fails through disobedience, and God chooses David — a man after His own heart.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–7** — Samuel's birth and call (1–3); the ark captured and returned (4–6); revival at Mizpah
- **Ch. 8–12** — Israel demands a king; Saul anointed and confirmed
- **Ch. 13–15** — Saul's disobedience and rejection
- **Ch. 16–17** — David anointed (16); David and Goliath (17)
- **Ch. 18–27** — Saul's jealousy; David's flight; David spares Saul twice (24, 26)
- **Ch. 28–31** — The medium at En-dor; Saul's death at Gilboa

---

### 10. 2 Samuel (24 chapters)
**Summary:** David's reign — his triumphs, God's covenant promise of an eternal throne, his sin with Bathsheba, and the painful consequences that fracture his family and kingdom.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–4** — David king over Judah; civil war with Saul's house
- **Ch. 5–10** — David king over all Israel; Jerusalem captured; the ark brought up (6); the Davidic covenant (7); victories
- **Ch. 11–12** — David, Bathsheba, and Uriah; Nathan's rebuke
- **Ch. 13–18** — Family tragedy: Amnon and Tamar; Absalom's revolt and death
- **Ch. 19–24** — Restoration; Sheba's revolt; David's song (22); mighty men; the census and the threshing floor (24)

---

### 11. 1 Kings (22 chapters)
**Summary:** Solomon's golden age and temple give way to a divided kingdom; a parade of kings mostly do evil, and the prophet Elijah confronts idolatry in the north.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–4** — Solomon secures the throne; his wisdom
- **Ch. 5–8** — Building and dedication of the temple
- **Ch. 9–11** — Solomon's splendor, compromise, and fall
- **Ch. 12–16** — The kingdom divides (Rehoboam/Jeroboam); kings of Israel and Judah
- **Ch. 17–19** — Elijah: the drought, Mount Carmel (18), the still small voice at Horeb (19)
- **Ch. 20–22** — Ahab's wars; Naboth's vineyard (21); Ahab's death

---

### 12. 2 Kings (25 chapters)
**Summary:** Elisha's ministry, continued royal failure, and the fall of both kingdoms: Israel to Assyria (722 BC) and Judah to Babylon (586 BC), with the temple destroyed and the people exiled.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–2** — Elijah taken up; Elisha succeeds him
- **Ch. 3–8** — Elisha's miracles: the widow's oil, the Shunammite, Naaman healed (5)
- **Ch. 9–13** — Jehu's purge of Ahab's house; Joash repairs the temple
- **Ch. 14–17** — Decline of Israel; fall of Samaria to Assyria (17)
- **Ch. 18–20** — Hezekiah's reforms; Sennacherib's siege turned back (18–19)
- **Ch. 21–23** — Manasseh's evil; Josiah's reforms and the rediscovered Law (22–23)
- **Ch. 24–25** — Fall of Jerusalem to Babylon; exile

---

### 13. 1 Chronicles (29 chapters)
**Summary:** A post-exilic retelling of Israel's story focused on David, the temple, and proper worship — reminding the returned exiles who they are.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–9** — Genealogies from Adam through the returned exiles
- **Ch. 10–12** — Saul's death; David becomes king; his warriors
- **Ch. 13–16** — Bringing the ark to Jerusalem; worship established
- **Ch. 17** — The Davidic covenant
- **Ch. 18–20** — David's victories
- **Ch. 21–29** — Census; preparations for the temple; organization of priests, Levites, and officials; David's charge to Solomon and prayer

---

### 14. 2 Chronicles (36 chapters)
**Summary:** From Solomon's temple to the exile, told through Judah's kings alone — highlighting reformers who sought the LORD, and ending with hope: Cyrus's decree to return and rebuild.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–9** — Solomon's reign and the temple (dedication in 6–7; "if my people..." 7:14)
- **Ch. 10–12** — Division of the kingdom; Rehoboam
- **Ch. 13–28** — Judah's kings: Asa, Jehoshaphat (17–20), Joash, Uzziah (26), Ahaz
- **Ch. 29–32** — Hezekiah's reforms and Passover
- **Ch. 33–35** — Manasseh's repentance; Josiah's reforms
- **Ch. 36** — Final kings; the fall of Jerusalem; Cyrus's decree

---

### 15. Ezra (10 chapters)
**Summary:** Exiles return from Babylon in two waves: Zerubbabel rebuilds the temple despite opposition, and Ezra the scribe returns to teach the Law and lead spiritual reform.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–2** — Cyrus's decree; the first return under Zerubbabel
- **Ch. 3–6** — Altar and temple rebuilt; opposition; temple completed and dedicated (515 BC)
- **Ch. 7–8** — Ezra's return and mission
- **Ch. 9–10** — The intermarriage crisis; confession and reform

---

### 16. Nehemiah (13 chapters)
**Summary:** Nehemiah, cupbearer to the Persian king, returns to rebuild Jerusalem's walls in 52 days amid fierce opposition, then joins Ezra in covenant renewal and community reform.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–2** — Nehemiah's prayer and commission; night inspection of the walls
- **Ch. 3–6** — Rebuilding amid ridicule, threats, and internal injustice; wall completed
- **Ch. 7** — Census of the returnees
- **Ch. 8–10** — Ezra reads the Law; confession; covenant renewed
- **Ch. 11–13** — Resettlement; wall dedication; Nehemiah's final reforms

---

### 17. Esther (10 chapters)
**Summary:** In Persia, a Jewish orphan becomes queen and, at mortal risk, exposes Haman's genocidal plot — God's providence preserving His people, though His name is never mentioned.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–2** — Vashti deposed; Esther becomes queen; Mordecai foils a plot
- **Ch. 3–4** — Haman's decree to destroy the Jews; "for such a time as this" (4:14)
- **Ch. 5–7** — Esther's banquets; Haman's downfall on his own gallows
- **Ch. 8–10** — The Jews delivered; the feast of Purim established

---
## The Poetic & Wisdom Books

---

### 18. Job (42 chapters)
**Summary:** A righteous man loses everything and wrestles with why the innocent suffer. His friends' tidy answers fail; God answers from the whirlwind, and Job is humbled and restored.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–2** — Prologue: heavenly scenes; Job's losses and endurance
- **Ch. 3** — Job's lament
- **Ch. 4–27** — Three cycles of dialogue with Eliphaz, Bildad, and Zophar
- **Ch. 28** — Poem on wisdom
- **Ch. 29–31** — Job's final defense
- **Ch. 32–37** — Elihu's speeches
- **Ch. 38–41** — The LORD answers from the whirlwind
- **Ch. 42** — Job's repentance and restoration

---

### 19. Psalms (150 chapters)
**Summary:** Israel's inspired hymnbook and prayer book — 150 songs of praise, lament, thanksgiving, confession, royal hope, and wisdom, arranged in five books.

**Outline (the five books):**
- **Book I: Ps 1–41** — Mostly Davidic; the blessed man (1), the anointed Son (2), the shepherd psalm (23), confession (32)
- **Book II: Ps 42–72** — Longing for God (42), penitence (51), ending with the royal psalm (72)
- **Book III: Ps 73–89** — Asaph and crisis psalms; the anguished ending over the Davidic covenant (89)
- **Book IV: Ps 90–106** — "The LORD reigns"; Moses' prayer (90), refuge (91), praise psalms (95–100), steadfast love (103)
- **Book V: Ps 107–150** — Thanksgiving and ascent: the Messiah at God's right hand (110), the Torah psalm (119), Songs of Ascents (120–134), inescapable God (139), final hallelujahs (146–150)

---

### 20. Proverbs (31 chapters)
**Summary:** Practical wisdom for everyday life, grounded in the fear of the LORD — on speech, work, money, friendship, family, and character.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–9** — A father's lectures: wisdom vs. folly personified; "the fear of the LORD is the beginning of knowledge" (1:7)
- **Ch. 10–22:16** — Proverbs of Solomon (short two-line sayings)
- **Ch. 22:17–24** — Sayings of the wise
- **Ch. 25–29** — More proverbs of Solomon, copied by Hezekiah's men
- **Ch. 30** — Sayings of Agur
- **Ch. 31** — Sayings of King Lemuel; the excellent wife (31:10–31)

---

### 21. Ecclesiastes (12 chapters)
**Summary:** The Preacher tests everything "under the sun" — pleasure, work, wealth, wisdom — and finds it fleeting vapor. Conclusion: fear God, keep His commandments, and receive life's simple gifts with joy.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–2** — "Vanity of vanities"; the experiment with pleasure and achievement
- **Ch. 3–6** — A time for everything (3); injustice, toil, wealth's emptiness
- **Ch. 7–11** — Wisdom's limits; enjoying life amid uncertainty; cast your bread on the waters
- **Ch. 12** — Remember your Creator; the end of the matter: fear God (12:13–14)

---

### 22. Song of Solomon (8 chapters)
**Summary:** A lyrical celebration of romantic love, desire, and marital delight between a bride and her beloved.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–2** — Mutual longing and admiration
- **Ch. 3–4** — Wedding procession; the groom's praise of the bride
- **Ch. 5–6** — Separation, searching, and reunion
- **Ch. 7–8** — Love's delight and strength: "love is strong as death" (8:6–7)

---

## The Major Prophets

---

### 23. Isaiah (66 chapters)
**Summary:** Judgment and hope on a grand scale: God will judge Judah's sin and the nations' pride, yet through the coming Servant-King He will redeem His people and renew all creation.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–12** — Indictment of Judah; Isaiah's call (6); Immanuel (7:14); the child born to us (9:6); the branch of Jesse (11)
- **Ch. 13–27** — Oracles against the nations; apocalyptic hope
- **Ch. 28–35** — Woes and the promise of a righteous King
- **Ch. 36–39** — Historical interlude: Hezekiah and Assyria
- **Ch. 40–48** — Comfort for the exiles; God's incomparable greatness; Cyrus foretold
- **Ch. 49–55** — The Servant songs climaxing in the suffering Servant (52:13–53:12); free grace (55)
- **Ch. 56–66** — True worship; the anointed preacher (61); new heavens and new earth (65–66)

---

### 24. Jeremiah (52 chapters)
**Summary:** The "weeping prophet" pleads with Judah for forty years to repent before Babylon comes; he suffers rejection, watches Jerusalem fall, yet proclaims a new covenant written on the heart.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1** — Jeremiah's call
- **Ch. 2–25** — Sermons of warning: broken cisterns (2), temple sermon (7), the potter (18), seventy years of exile (25)
- **Ch. 26–29** — Conflict with false prophets; letter to the exiles (29:11)
- **Ch. 30–33** — The Book of Consolation: restoration and the new covenant (31:31–34)
- **Ch. 34–45** — Jerusalem's last days; Jeremiah imprisoned; the fall (39); aftermath in Egypt
- **Ch. 46–51** — Oracles against the nations, ending with Babylon
- **Ch. 52** — Historical appendix: the fall of Jerusalem

---

### 25. Lamentations (5 chapters)
**Summary:** Five acrostic poems of grief over Jerusalem's destruction — raw sorrow anchored by one hope at the center: God's mercies are new every morning.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1** — The desolate city weeps
- **Ch. 2** — The LORD's anger against Zion
- **Ch. 3** — Personal anguish and the turning point of hope (3:22–24)
- **Ch. 4** — The siege's horrors remembered
- **Ch. 5** — A communal prayer for restoration

---

### 26. Ezekiel (48 chapters)
**Summary:** A priest-prophet among the exiles sees God's glory depart the temple in judgment — then promises a new heart, resurrection for a dead nation, and glory returning to a new temple.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–3** — The throne-chariot vision; Ezekiel's call
- **Ch. 4–24** — Sign-acts and oracles of judgment on Jerusalem; the glory departs (10–11); personal responsibility (18)
- **Ch. 25–32** — Oracles against the nations (including Tyre and Egypt)
- **Ch. 33–39** — Restoration: the watchman (33), the true Shepherd (34), a new heart and Spirit (36:26–27), the valley of dry bones (37), Gog (38–39)
- **Ch. 40–48** — Vision of the new temple; the river of life (47); "The LORD Is There" (48:35)

---

### 27. Daniel (12 chapters)
**Summary:** Faithful Jewish exiles thrive in pagan courts as God humbles kings and reveals, through dreams and visions, that His kingdom will outlast every empire.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1** — Daniel and friends in Babylon's court
- **Ch. 2** — Nebuchadnezzar's statue dream; the stone-kingdom
- **Ch. 3** — The fiery furnace
- **Ch. 4** — Nebuchadnezzar humbled
- **Ch. 5** — The writing on the wall; Babylon falls
- **Ch. 6** — Daniel in the lions' den
- **Ch. 7–8** — Visions: four beasts and the Son of Man (7); ram and goat (8)
- **Ch. 9** — Daniel's prayer; the seventy weeks
- **Ch. 10–12** — Final vision: kings of north and south; resurrection hope (12:2–3)

---

## The Minor Prophets

---

### 28. Hosea (14 chapters)
**Summary:** Hosea's marriage to unfaithful Gomer becomes a living picture of God's wounded, relentless love for adulterous Israel.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–3** — Hosea, Gomer, and the children; redemption of the unfaithful wife
- **Ch. 4–10** — Israel's charges: no knowledge of God, idolatry, false security
- **Ch. 11–14** — God's fatherly compassion (11); call to return; promise of healing (14)

---

### 29. Joel (3 chapters)
**Summary:** A devastating locust plague becomes a preview of the day of the LORD — a call to repentance, with the promise that God will pour out His Spirit on all flesh.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1** — The locust devastation
- **Ch. 2** — The day of the LORD; "rend your hearts"; the Spirit poured out (2:28–32)
- **Ch. 3** — Judgment of the nations; blessing for Zion

---

### 30. Amos (9 chapters)
**Summary:** A shepherd from Judah thunders against Israel's injustice and hollow religion: "Let justice roll down like waters."

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–2** — Judgments on the nations, closing in on Israel
- **Ch. 3–6** — Sermons of indictment: oppression of the poor, complacent luxury, despised worship (5:21–24)
- **Ch. 7–9** — Five visions of judgment; Amos vs. Amaziah; the restored booth of David (9:11–15)

---

### 31. Obadiah (1 chapter)
**Summary:** The shortest Old Testament book: judgment on Edom for gloating over Jerusalem's fall, and the promise that the kingdom will be the LORD's.

**Outline:**
- **vv. 1–14** — Edom's pride and betrayal of his brother Jacob
- **vv. 15–21** — The day of the LORD; Zion's deliverance

---

### 32. Jonah (4 chapters)
**Summary:** A prophet flees God's call to Nineveh, is swallowed by a great fish, reluctantly preaches, and sulks when the enemy city repents — exposing God's mercy and Jonah's heart.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1** — Jonah flees; the storm; thrown into the sea
- **Ch. 2** — Prayer from the fish's belly
- **Ch. 3** — Nineveh repents
- **Ch. 4** — Jonah's anger; God's question of compassion

---

### 33. Micah (7 chapters)
**Summary:** Alternating judgment and hope: God prosecutes His people for injustice, promises a ruler from Bethlehem, and defines true religion — do justice, love kindness, walk humbly.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–2** — Judgment on Samaria and Judah
- **Ch. 3–5** — Corrupt leaders; the mountain of the LORD (4); the ruler from Bethlehem (5:2)
- **Ch. 6–7** — God's lawsuit; what the LORD requires (6:8); who is a God like You? (7:18–20)

---

### 34. Nahum (3 chapters)
**Summary:** About a century after Jonah, Nineveh's repentance has faded — Nahum announces the certain fall of the brutal Assyrian empire, a comfort to the oppressed.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1** — The LORD: jealous, avenging, a stronghold for those who trust Him
- **Ch. 2** — The siege and fall of Nineveh
- **Ch. 3** — Woe to the bloody city

---

### 35. Habakkuk (3 chapters)
**Summary:** A prophet questions God: Why does evil go unpunished — and why use wicked Babylon to judge Judah? God answers: "The righteous shall live by his faith."

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1** — Habakkuk's two complaints
- **Ch. 2** — God's answer: the vision awaits; five woes on Babylon; 2:4
- **Ch. 3** — Habakkuk's prayer-psalm; joy in God despite ruin (3:17–19)

---

### 36. Zephaniah (3 chapters)
**Summary:** The great day of the LORD is near — sweeping judgment on Judah and the nations, followed by a purified remnant and God rejoicing over His people with singing.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1** — The coming day of wrath
- **Ch. 2** — Seek the LORD; oracles against the nations
- **Ch. 3** — Jerusalem's corruption; restoration and divine joy (3:17)

---

### 37. Haggai (2 chapters)
**Summary:** After the return from exile, the people have stalled on rebuilding the temple. Haggai's four dated messages stir them to put God's house first.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1** — "Consider your ways" — work resumes on the temple
- **Ch. 2** — The latter glory greater than the former; blessing from this day; Zerubbabel the signet ring

---

### 38. Zechariah (14 chapters)
**Summary:** Visions and oracles encouraging the temple builders, pointing beyond to a humble King on a donkey, a pierced Shepherd, and the LORD reigning over all the earth.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–6** — Eight night visions (lampstand: "not by might... but by my Spirit," 4:6); the crowned Branch
- **Ch. 7–8** — Questions about fasting; promises of Zion's future
- **Ch. 9–11** — First oracle: the King on a donkey (9:9); the rejected Shepherd
- **Ch. 12–14** — Second oracle: "they will look on me, whom they have pierced" (12:10); the LORD king over all (14:9)

---

### 39. Malachi (4 chapters)
**Summary:** The Old Testament's closing word: God confronts post-exilic complacency through six disputes, and promises the messenger who prepares the way before the day of the LORD.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1** — "I have loved you"; polluted offerings
- **Ch. 2** — Corrupt priests; faithless marriages
- **Ch. 3** — The coming messenger (3:1); robbing God in tithes; the book of remembrance
- **Ch. 4** — The day coming; the sun of righteousness; Elijah promised

---

# THE NEW TESTAMENT

## The Gospels & Acts

---

### 40. Matthew (28 chapters)
**Summary:** Jesus is Israel's Messiah, the son of David and Abraham, the true King who fulfills the Old Testament, teaches the way of the kingdom, dies for sins, and commissions His church to disciple all nations.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–2** — Genealogy, virgin birth, the magi, flight to Egypt
- **Ch. 3–4** — John the Baptist; Jesus' baptism and temptation; ministry begins
- **Ch. 5–7** — The Sermon on the Mount (Beatitudes, Lord's Prayer, golden rule)
- **Ch. 8–9** — Miracles of authority
- **Ch. 10** — The Twelve sent out
- **Ch. 11–12** — Responses to Jesus; conflict with Pharisees
- **Ch. 13** — Kingdom parables (sower, weeds, mustard seed, pearl)
- **Ch. 14–17** — Feeding the 5,000; Peter's confession (16); transfiguration (17)
- **Ch. 18** — Life in the community: humility, forgiveness
- **Ch. 19–23** — Journey to Jerusalem; triumphal entry (21); woes on the scribes and Pharisees (23)
- **Ch. 24–25** — The Olivet Discourse: the end of the age; parables of readiness
- **Ch. 26–27** — Last Supper, Gethsemane, trials, crucifixion
- **Ch. 28** — Resurrection; the Great Commission (28:18–20)

---

### 41. Mark (16 chapters)
**Summary:** The fast-paced Gospel of Jesus the Servant-King: the Son of God who came "not to be served but to serve, and to give his life as a ransom for many" (10:45).

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–3** — Ministry launched in Galilee: baptism, calling disciples, healings, controversy
- **Ch. 4–8:26** — Parables and mighty works: calming the storm (4), Legion (5), feeding multitudes (6, 8)
- **Ch. 8:27–10** — Turning point: Peter's confession; three passion predictions; discipleship as cross-bearing
- **Ch. 11–13** — Jerusalem: triumphal entry, temple cleansing, debates, Olivet Discourse
- **Ch. 14–15** — Passion: Last Supper, arrest, trials, crucifixion; the centurion's confession (15:39)
- **Ch. 16** — The empty tomb

---

### 42. Luke (24 chapters)
**Summary:** A carefully researched account of Jesus the Savior of all people — with special attention to the poor, women, outsiders, prayer, and the Spirit. "The Son of Man came to seek and to save the lost" (19:10).

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–2** — Births of John and Jesus; Mary's song; the shepherds; boy Jesus in the temple
- **Ch. 3–4** — Baptism, genealogy, temptation; the Nazareth sermon (4:18–19)
- **Ch. 5–9:50** — Galilean ministry: calling sinners, sermon on the plain (6), forgiveness (7), transfiguration (9)
- **Ch. 9:51–19:27** — The journey to Jerusalem: good Samaritan (10), Lord's Prayer (11), rich fool (12), lost sheep/coin/son (15), rich man and Lazarus (16), ten lepers (17), Zacchaeus (19)
- **Ch. 19:28–21** — Jerusalem: entry, temple, controversies, Olivet Discourse
- **Ch. 22–23** — Last Supper, arrest, trials, crucifixion; the penitent thief
- **Ch. 24** — Resurrection; the Emmaus road; ascension

---

### 43. John (21 chapters)
**Summary:** The Word made flesh: seven signs and seven "I am" sayings reveal Jesus as the divine Son, "that you may believe... and that by believing you may have life in his name" (20:31).

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1** — Prologue: the Word was God; first disciples
- **Ch. 2–4** — Water to wine; Nicodemus and new birth (3:16); the Samaritan woman
- **Ch. 5–10** — Signs and disputes: healing at Bethesda, bread of life (6), light of the world (8), man born blind (9), good shepherd (10)
- **Ch. 11–12** — Raising of Lazarus; anointing; triumphal entry
- **Ch. 13–17** — The upper room: foot washing, new commandment, the way/truth/life (14), the vine (15), the Spirit promised (14–16), the high priestly prayer (17)
- **Ch. 18–19** — Arrest, trials before Pilate, crucifixion — "It is finished"
- **Ch. 20–21** — Resurrection: Mary, Thomas; restoration of Peter

---

### 44. Acts (28 chapters)
**Summary:** The risen Jesus pours out the Spirit, and the gospel spreads from Jerusalem to Rome — through the witness of Peter, the martyrdom of Stephen, the conversion of Paul, and three missionary journeys.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–2** — Ascension; Pentecost and Peter's sermon; the first church
- **Ch. 3–7** — Witness in Jerusalem: healings, persecution, Ananias and Sapphira, Stephen's martyrdom
- **Ch. 8–12** — Expansion: Samaria and the Ethiopian (8), Saul converted (9), Cornelius and the Gentiles (10–11), Peter freed (12)
- **Ch. 13–14** — First missionary journey (Paul and Barnabas)
- **Ch. 15** — The Jerusalem council: salvation by grace for Gentiles
- **Ch. 16–18** — Second journey: Philippi, Thessalonica, Athens (17), Corinth
- **Ch. 18–21** — Third journey: Ephesus (19); farewell to the elders (20)
- **Ch. 21–26** — Paul arrested in Jerusalem; defenses before rulers
- **Ch. 27–28** — Voyage and shipwreck; Rome — the gospel unhindered

---

## The Epistles of Paul

---

### 45. Romans (16 chapters)
**Summary:** Paul's fullest exposition of the gospel: all have sinned; all are justified freely by grace through faith in Christ; nothing can separate believers from God's love; therefore live transformed lives.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–3** — The universal problem: Gentile and Jew alike under sin (3:23)
- **Ch. 3:21–5** — Justification by faith; Abraham's example; peace with God; Adam and Christ
- **Ch. 6–8** — United to Christ: dead to sin, free from law's condemnation, life in the Spirit; no separation (8:38–39)
- **Ch. 9–11** — Israel's place in God's plan
- **Ch. 12–15** — Living sacrifice; love, government, conscience, the strong and the weak
- **Ch. 16** — Greetings and doxology

---

### 46. 1 Corinthians (16 chapters)
**Summary:** Paul confronts a divided, gifted, morally confused church — applying the wisdom of the cross to factions, immorality, lawsuits, marriage, worship, spiritual gifts, love, and resurrection.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–4** — Divisions; the foolish wisdom of the cross
- **Ch. 5–6** — Discipline of immorality; lawsuits; the body as the Spirit's temple
- **Ch. 7** — Marriage and singleness
- **Ch. 8–10** — Food offered to idols; freedom and love
- **Ch. 11–14** — Worship: the Lord's Supper (11), spiritual gifts (12), the love chapter (13), order and edification (14)
- **Ch. 15** — The resurrection of Christ and of believers
- **Ch. 16** — The collection; final words

---

### 47. 2 Corinthians (13 chapters)
**Summary:** Paul's most personal letter: defending his ministry against critics, he reveals the paradox of gospel service — treasure in jars of clay, strength perfected in weakness.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–7** — Ministry explained: comfort in affliction, the new covenant's glory (3), jars of clay (4), new creation and reconciliation (5), joy over their repentance
- **Ch. 8–9** — The grace of generous giving
- **Ch. 10–13** — Paul's defense: boasting in weakness, the thorn in the flesh (12:9–10)

---

### 48. Galatians (6 chapters)
**Summary:** A fiery defense of the gospel of grace: justification is by faith in Christ, not works of the law — so stand firm in freedom and walk by the Spirit.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–2** — No other gospel; Paul's apostleship; crucified with Christ (2:20)
- **Ch. 3–4** — Faith vs. law: Abraham, the curse, sons and heirs
- **Ch. 5–6** — Freedom in Christ; the fruit of the Spirit (5:22–23); bearing burdens

---

### 49. Ephesians (6 chapters)
**Summary:** God's cosmic plan to unite all things in Christ: saved by grace, Jew and Gentile made one new humanity, the church equipped to walk worthy and stand in spiritual armor.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–3** — Riches in Christ: election and redemption (1), saved by grace through faith (2:8–9), the mystery of the united church (3)
- **Ch. 4–6** — Walking worthy: unity and gifts (4), love and light, marriage and family (5), the armor of God (6:10–18)

---

### 50. Philippians (4 chapters)
**Summary:** A joyful thank-you letter from prison: to live is Christ; have the humble mind of Christ; press on toward the goal; rejoice in the Lord always.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1** — Partnership in the gospel; to live is Christ (1:21)
- **Ch. 2** — The Christ hymn: humility and exaltation (2:5–11)
- **Ch. 3** — Righteousness from God by faith; pressing toward the goal
- **Ch. 4** — Rejoice; the peace of God; contentment; God supplies every need

---

### 51. Colossians (4 chapters)
**Summary:** Against a philosophy diminishing Christ, Paul exalts Him as supreme over creation and the church — believers are complete in Him and should seek the things above.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1** — The supremacy of Christ (1:15–20)
- **Ch. 2** — Fullness in Christ; warnings against hollow philosophy and legalism
- **Ch. 3** — Raised with Christ: put off the old, put on the new; household relationships
- **Ch. 4** — Prayer, gracious speech, final greetings

---

### 52. 1 Thessalonians (5 chapters)
**Summary:** Encouragement for a young persecuted church: Paul recalls their conversion, urges holy living and love, and comforts them about believers who have died — the Lord will return.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–3** — Thanksgiving for their faith; Paul's ministry among them; Timothy's good report
- **Ch. 4** — Sanctification; the dead in Christ will rise (4:13–18)
- **Ch. 5** — The day of the Lord; final exhortations (rejoice, pray, give thanks)

---

### 53. 2 Thessalonians (3 chapters)
**Summary:** Correcting confusion about the day of the Lord: it has not yet come; stand firm under persecution, and don't be idle while waiting.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1** — Comfort in persecution; coming judgment
- **Ch. 2** — The man of lawlessness must come first; stand firm
- **Ch. 3** — Warning against idleness; work quietly

---

### 54. 1 Timothy (6 chapters)
**Summary:** Instructions to Paul's young delegate in Ephesus: guard sound doctrine, order the church's worship and leadership, and set an example of godliness.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1** — Charge against false teachers; Christ came to save sinners
- **Ch. 2–3** — Prayer and worship; qualifications for overseers and deacons
- **Ch. 4** — Train yourself for godliness; set an example
- **Ch. 5** — Care for widows, elders, and the church family
- **Ch. 6** — Contentment vs. love of money; fight the good fight

---

### 55. 2 Timothy (4 chapters)
**Summary:** Paul's final letter, written facing death: guard the gospel, endure suffering, preach the word — "I have fought the good fight, I have finished the race."

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1** — Fan into flame your gift; unashamed of the gospel
- **Ch. 2** — A good soldier; the faithful saying; a worker approved
- **Ch. 3** — Last-days difficulty; all Scripture is God-breathed (3:16–17)
- **Ch. 4** — Preach the word; Paul's farewell (4:6–8)

---

### 56. Titus (3 chapters)
**Summary:** Instructions for organizing churches in Crete: appoint qualified elders, teach sound doctrine, and let grace train God's people to live godly, good-works-filled lives.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1** — Elders appointed; false teachers silenced
- **Ch. 2** — Sound teaching for every age group; grace that trains us (2:11–14)
- **Ch. 3** — Devoted to good works; saved by mercy, washed and renewed (3:4–7)

---

### 57. Philemon (1 chapter)
**Summary:** A personal appeal: Paul asks Philemon to receive back his runaway slave Onesimus — now a brother in Christ — charging any debt to Paul's own account. The gospel transforming relationships.

**Outline:**
- **vv. 1–7** — Greeting and thanksgiving for Philemon's love
- **vv. 8–22** — The appeal for Onesimus: "no longer as a bondservant but... a beloved brother"
- **vv. 23–25** — Final greetings

---

## The General Epistles & Revelation

---

### 58. Hebrews (13 chapters)
**Summary:** Jesus is better — greater than angels, Moses, and the old priesthood; His once-for-all sacrifice fulfills the entire sacrificial system. So don't drift back: hold fast, and run the race by faith.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–2** — The Son superior to angels; made like His brothers
- **Ch. 3–4** — Greater than Moses; entering God's rest; the living word (4:12)
- **Ch. 5–7** — The great high priest after the order of Melchizedek
- **Ch. 8–10** — The new covenant; the true sanctuary; one sacrifice for sins forever; draw near, hold fast
- **Ch. 11** — The hall of faith
- **Ch. 12** — Run the race looking to Jesus; discipline; an unshakable kingdom
- **Ch. 13** — Practical exhortations; the great Shepherd

---

### 59. James (5 chapters)
**Summary:** Wisdom for practical faith: trials, temptation, the tongue, favoritism, wealth, and prayer — because faith without works is dead.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1** — Joy in trials; be doers of the word
- **Ch. 2** — No partiality; faith and works
- **Ch. 3** — Taming the tongue; wisdom from above
- **Ch. 4** — Quarrels, worldliness, humility; "if the Lord wills"
- **Ch. 5** — Warning to the rich; patience; the prayer of faith

---

### 60. 1 Peter (5 chapters)
**Summary:** Hope for "elect exiles" suffering for their faith: born again to a living hope, called to holy living, honorable conduct, and joyful endurance after Christ's example.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1** — A living hope; an imperishable inheritance; be holy
- **Ch. 2** — Living stones and a royal priesthood; Christ's example in suffering
- **Ch. 3** — Marriage, blessing under persecution, a defense of hope (3:15)
- **Ch. 4** — Suffering as a Christian; stewarding grace
- **Ch. 5** — Shepherd the flock; humility; cast your anxieties on Him

---

### 61. 2 Peter (3 chapters)
**Summary:** Peter's farewell: grow in godliness, beware false teachers, and remember — the Lord's apparent delay is patience; the day of the Lord will come.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1** — Growth in grace; the sure prophetic word
- **Ch. 2** — False teachers described and doomed
- **Ch. 3** — Scoffers; the day of the Lord; new heavens and new earth

---

### 62. 1 John (5 chapters)
**Summary:** Tests of authentic life with God — walking in the light, loving one another, and believing Jesus is the Christ come in the flesh — "that you may know that you have eternal life."

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1–2** — Light and confession; the new-old commandment; antichrists
- **Ch. 3** — Children of God; love in deed and truth
- **Ch. 4** — Test the spirits; God is love (4:7–12)
- **Ch. 5** — Faith that overcomes; assurance of eternal life (5:13)

---

### 63. 2 John (1 chapter)
**Summary:** Walk in truth and love — and do not welcome or support teachers who deny Christ's coming in the flesh.

**Outline:**
- **vv. 1–6** — Walking in truth and love
- **vv. 7–13** — Warning against deceivers; guarding the teaching

---

### 64. 3 John (1 chapter)
**Summary:** The shortest book of the Bible: praise for Gaius's hospitality to traveling gospel workers, contrasted with domineering Diotrephes; imitate good, not evil.

**Outline:**
- **vv. 1–8** — Gaius commended for faithfulness and hospitality
- **vv. 9–12** — Diotrephes rebuked; Demetrius commended
- **vv. 13–15** — Closing hope to speak face to face

---

### 65. Jude (1 chapter)
**Summary:** An urgent appeal to contend for the faith against infiltrating false teachers — ending with one of Scripture's great doxologies to Him who is able to keep you from stumbling.

**Outline:**
- **vv. 1–4** — Contend for the faith once delivered
- **vv. 5–16** — False teachers: past judgments and vivid descriptions
- **vv. 17–25** — Keep yourselves in God's love; show mercy; doxology

---

### 66. Revelation (22 chapters)
**Summary:** Jesus unveils the end of the story to John on Patmos: letters to seven churches, heavenly worship, cycles of judgment, the fall of Babylon, Christ's victorious return, final judgment, and a new heaven and new earth where God dwells with His people forever.

**Outline:**
- **Ch. 1** — Vision of the glorified Christ
- **Ch. 2–3** — Letters to the seven churches (Ephesus through Laodicea)
- **Ch. 4–5** — The throne room; the Lamb who was slain takes the scroll
- **Ch. 6–8:1** — The seven seals
- **Ch. 8–11** — The seven trumpets; the two witnesses
- **Ch. 12–14** — The woman, the dragon, and the beasts; the Lamb and the 144,000
- **Ch. 15–16** — The seven bowls of wrath
- **Ch. 17–18** — The fall of Babylon the great
- **Ch. 19** — The marriage supper of the Lamb; the rider on the white horse
- **Ch. 20** — The thousand years; the great white throne judgment
- **Ch. 21–22** — New heaven and new earth; the new Jerusalem; "Behold, I am making all things new"; "Come, Lord Jesus"
